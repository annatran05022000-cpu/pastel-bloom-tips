## Done-for-You Lead Generation Agencies

### What You Get

A lead gen agency assigns a team to manage your outreach end-to-end: list building, copywriting, sending, and reply handling. You receive booked meetings, not leads.

### Average Cost

$4,000-$12,000/month for a dedicated SDR (or equivalent agency service). Most contracts are 3-6 months minimum.

### When It Makes Sense

- You have zero internal bandwidth for prospecting
- Your deal size justifies $5K+/month acquisition cost (typically $20K+ ACV)
- You need results in 30-60 days, not 90+

### When to Avoid

- Early-stage: before you've validated your ICP and messaging yourself
- Low ACV: if your average deal is under $5,000, agency cost rarely pencils out
- When you need to learn: outsourcing outreach means losing the institutional knowledge of what works for your ICP

**Representative agencies:** Belkins, CIENCE Technologies, Martal Group

## Questions We Get Often

**How do I handle out-of-office replies?**

Most outreach tools automatically pause sequences for contacts with OOO replies and resume after the specified return date. For contacts that go OOO, delay the next touch by 2-3 days after their stated return date. Don't remove them — they were active enough to set an OOO, which means they're functional contacts.

**What subject lines work best for B2B cold email in 2026?**

Highest-performing patterns: (1) "[Their company] + [specific observation]" — personalized, curiosity-driving. (2) Question format — "Quick question about [their team/challenge]". (3) Referral format — "[Mutual contact] suggested I reach out". Avoid: "Following up", "Quick question" with no specifics, anything that sounds like a newsletter.

**Should I A/B test everything?**

No — test one variable at a time with sufficient volume. Subject line variations need 200+ sends per variant to be statistically meaningful. Sequence length changes need 500+ contacts. Body copy changes are hard to isolate because multiple variables change simultaneously. Pick the highest-leverage variable (usually subject line first, then opening sentence) and run clean tests.

**How do I keep my sending domain out of spam filters?**

Five practices: (1) Keep bounce rate under 3% — [GetLeadSnap](https://getleadsnap.pro) verification handles this. (2) Warm new domains for 4-6 weeks before full volume. (3) Maintain proper SPF, DKIM, and DMARC records. (4) Avoid spam trigger words. (5) Keep HTML minimal in cold emails — plain text outperforms styled templates.

**What's the best CRM for tracking B2B outreach results?**

For teams under 20 people: HubSpot Free. It handles contact tracking, pipeline management, and basic reporting at zero cost. For growing teams: Pipedrive or Close.io. For enterprise: Salesforce. The most important feature isn't functionality — it's adoption. The best CRM is the one your team actually uses.

The most common mistake in B2B outreach isn't bad messaging — it's treating it like a campaign. Campaigns have a start date and an end date. Systems run continuously.

**What a system looks like in practice:**

- New contacts added to the pipeline every week, not during "outreach pushes"
- Message optimization running continuously — 2-3 active A/B tests at any given time

Organizations that approach lead generation systemically consistently outperform those chasing tactics. ## Questions We Get Often

**How long does it take to see results from a new lead generation program?**

Most teams see their first meetings booked within 2-3 weeks of launching a properly set up outreach sequence with verified contact data. Meaningful pipeline — enough to influence revenue forecasting — typically takes 60-90 days to build. The lag exists because sales cycles don't compress just because you're generating more leads.

The variable that most affects time-to-results is data quality. Teams using verified contacts from platforms like [GetLeadSnap](https://getleadsnap.pro) consistently report faster ramp times than teams working with unverified lists.

**What's a realistic cost per lead for B2B outreach?**

For cold email outreach targeting US SMBs, a realistic cost per lead (meeting booked) ranges from $15-60 depending on your ICP, message quality, and the cost of your contact data platform. This compares favorably to paid channels where B2B lead costs typically range from $80-200+.

The math: if your contact data costs $200/month and you book 8 meetings, your CPL is $25. Add your time cost (5 hours/week × $50/hour = $200) and you're at $50/meeting — still strong ROI for deals above $3,000.

**How do I know if my targeting is too broad or too narrow?**

A practical test: describe your ideal prospect in one sentence without using the word "companies." If you can't, your ICP is too broad. If the sentence is so specific that fewer than 500 businesses in the US match, it's too narrow.

The Goldilocks zone for most SMB-focused businesses: 5,000-50,000 addressable prospects in the US. Enough to run meaningful outreach for 12+ months without exhausting the market.

**Should I use phone, email, or LinkedIn as my primary channel?**

Start with email. It's the most scalable, measurable, and improvable channel. Add LinkedIn once you've validated your messaging (LinkedIn amplifies good messages but doesn't fix bad ones). Add phone for high-value prospects who engage but don't reply.

The sequencing matters: email first lets you test and iterate cheaply before committing to higher-touch channels.

**How many follow-ups are too many?**

The research is clear: most teams follow up too few times, not too many. Seven touches over 25 days is the professional standard. Beyond that, monthly check-ins for 3-6 months keep you on the radar without becoming annoying.

The key is relevance. A follow-up with a new piece of information ("saw you just closed a Series A — congrats, curious if X is on the radar now") never feels like spam.
